## Part B - Survey Evaluation:

Identify and describe survey features:

```
Goal of survey: Explore Individual contributory behaviours in hope to inform governmental policy and program decisions related to charitable and volunteer sector.

Target population: all individuals 15 years of age and older living in one of the ten provinces of Canada, excluding individuals who reside in those institutions where they volunteer for more than 6 months.

Sampling frame: landline and cellular telephone numbers from Census and other adminsitrative sources with association with statistics Canada

Sampling Approach: Stratify sampling based on geographically defined area - provinces and census metropolitan level. Two-stage sampling where 1st stage is by groups of telephone number and second units are individuals within identified households.
In total, there was 27 strata. Rejective sampling was further utilized to select participants for the long or short interview. 

Sample size and survey mode: 50,000 field sample and 40,000 invitation letter sent out out with link to electronic survey, there is also option for phone interview.

Timeline: 2018-09-04 to 2018-12-28 (Approximately 3.5 months)

Response rate: 41.9%

Weights: each person represent 2% of population ( 1 person repsensent 50 persons in the population)

Data processing: Social Survey Processing Environment (SSPE) was used for processing. This is a generalized data processing system with standardized data processing procedure. For phone interview, the CATI data capture program was utilized to identify any outlier responses. Results were later checked manually.

Imputation: Imputation involved multiple steps but essentially, the system found other participants' records (donor records) with characteristics that closely matches that of the non-respondent (recipient record) using a scoring system. Within those with highest score, one will randomly be selected to fill in the missing values.

Sources of error: Sampling error - undercoverage as shown in the response rate.

Limitations and Bias: Although all provinces are included, sampling unit is based on phone number. Rural area or hosuehold with no telephone number may not be represented which can lead to selection bias as well. In addition, since the language for the survey is only in English and French, certain ethnic group may be missed. Linkage to personal information such as tax and household information likely contributed to the low response rate. Although that is a variable of consideration, perhaps it would be better to link just to respondent tax information rather than the entire household as these are sensitive information.

Links/Reference: https://www23.statcan.gc.ca/imdb/p2SV.pl?Function=getSurvey&SDDS=4430

```
